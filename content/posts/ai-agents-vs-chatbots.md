<h2 class="wp-block-heading">🤖 Chatbots in 2025: Still Useful, Still Limited</h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:heading {"level":3} -->
<h3 class="wp-block-heading">What is a Chatbot?</h3>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>A <strong>chatbot</strong> is a software application designed to simulate conversation with users, often pre-programmed to respond to specific queries. Think of customer service bots that handle return requests or FAQ bots embedded on websites.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:heading {"level":3} -->
<h3 class="wp-block-heading">Key Characteristics:</h3>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:list -->
<ul class="wp-block-list"><!-- wp:list-item -->
<li>Rule-based or NLP-driven</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li>Reactive, not proactive</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li>Often dependent on decision trees</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li>Deployed in narrow contexts</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item --></ul>
<!-- /wp:list -->

<!-- wp:heading {"level":3} -->
<h3 class="wp-block-heading">Use Cases:</h3>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:list -->
<ul class="wp-block-list"><!-- wp:list-item -->
<li>Customer support</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li>Lead qualification</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li>Booking assistance</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item --></ul>
<!-- /wp:list -->

<!-- wp:heading {"level":3} -->
<h3 class="wp-block-heading">Limitations:</h3>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Chatbots struggle with tasks that require memory, reasoning, or autonomy. They're great at helping you cancel an order, but not so much at suggesting a better product based on your history.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">🤖 AI Agents in 2025: Your Digital Colleague</h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:heading {"level":3} -->
<h3 class="wp-block-heading">What is an AI Agent?</h3>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>An <strong>AI agent</strong> is an autonomous system designed to <strong>perceive, reason, plan, and act</strong> toward achieving a goal. Unlike chatbots, agents can execute tasks independently, connect to APIs, write code, and even make complex decisions.</p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">🔍 FAQs</h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:heading {"level":3} -->
<h3 class="wp-block-heading">What is the difference between AI agents and chatbots?</h3>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>AI agents are autonomous, goal-oriented systems capable of executing complex, multi-step tasks. Chatbots are typically reactive, providing scripted or NLP-based responses to direct inputs.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:heading {"level":3} -->
<h3 class="wp-block-heading">Are AI agents replacing chatbots?</h3>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Not entirely. They serve different purposes. While chatbots handle basic interactions, AI agents are better suited for complex automation and decision-making.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:heading {"level":3} -->
<h3 class="wp-block-heading">What are the best AI agent tools in 2025?</h3>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Top tools include Auto-GPT, Devin by Cognition Labs, Cognosys, and AgentGPT. Many integrate with productivity platforms or codebases.</p>
